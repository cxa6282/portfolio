Constructing a MIPS Processor in VHDL

An on-going laboratory sequence of implementing a Pipelined MIPS Processor in VHDL and testing on the Basys3 FPGA.

A Pipelined MIPS Processor can be broken into five stages, as shown in the diagram above. The stages were completed in weekly or bi-weekly sections, with a report written at the end of each stage.

Using Xilinx Vivado Design Suite, the components created in each stage were developed in VHDL and simulated against a test bench to ensure correct implementation. The waveforms shown above are from the Instruction Decode stage post-implementation timing simulation.

Stages that could be loaded onto the 16-switch Basys3 FPGA were tested on the board, as well.

The above shows the board demo of the ALU for the execution stage. The ALU was scaled to 4-bit inputs so that it could be loaded onto the board. In the picture, the MULTU opcode is selected "0110" and the inputs are "0010" and "0011", or 2 times 3. The result, 6, is indicated by the last four LED's ("0110" == 6).

The Memory Stage is the next stage to develop.
